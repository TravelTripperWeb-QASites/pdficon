January 2015 Lovely Boutique Hotel

For those for whom such issues matter, you don't support some indifferent corporation staying at WJ. This hotel is privately owned, and while not quite mom and pop--too elegant for that--one receives a warm greeting.

I've read others describe the rooms as tiny, but while small, I found my double a good size for two. Ours had a wall-mounted TV with cable including HBO and free wifi. It featured elegant decor and a lovely bathroom with large shower and enormous mirror. The front desk provided maps at check-in, inquired about our day when we returned to the room at night, helped us print boarding passes for our flight home, and arranged our car service to the airport. Maids smiled and greeted us as we passed them in the halls. The lobby honored both Christmas and Hanukkah with a gorgeous Christmas tree in the corner and a lighted menorah in front of a mirror. The room sparkled with good feeling.

As to location, we walked everywhere we needed to reach on our weekend in mid-town, never once needing the subway or a taxi. We attended three Broadway plays, shopped 5th Avenue, visited the tree at Rockefeller Center, wandered around the Christmas market at Central Park, enjoyed the frenzy at Times Square, and ate at several great New York restaurants in the immediate vicinity of the hotel. The Maria Pia across the street from WJ offers outstanding Italian, and Restaurant Row on 46th between 8th and 9th Avenues featured great French at Le Rivage and delightful Spanish at Sangria 46. Like the hotel, the restaurants named provide a non-corporate, mom and pop feel.

The WJ never disappointed. We thank the staff and management for a perfect long weekend.

August 2014 Excellent Little Hotel with Fantastic Location
  • There's a lot to like about this little hotel. Perhaps the biggest highlight is its location. The WJ is half a block from the 50th st.
